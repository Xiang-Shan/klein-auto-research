Next Steps & References

This study's job — quantify whether tabular self-supervision pays on this data, and where the same trained artifact pays anyway — is done. The open questions for a follow-up, in priority order (from findings.md §7):

  1. TabPFN v2, zero-shot. The knowledge base's default first move under ~50k rows, and at 58,592 rows this dataset sits at the edge of TabPFN v2.5's envelope. One inference pass answers whether a pre-trained tabular foundation model beats the E2 MLP / tuned GBDT pair (0.6706 / 0.6701) with zero tuning — the highest information-per-second experiment available.
  2. MLP + GBDT cross-family soft vote. E2's MLP is a genuinely different inductive bias at GBDT strength — exactly the diversity the campaign's soft-vote doctrine wants, where the FT-Transformer failed its correlation gate. Check the out-of-fold prediction correlation first (add the learner only if < 0.95), then target the campaign's 0.6715 global best.
  3. Complete the transductive aside. dae.py ships fit_mode="transductive" (the Jahrer-style train+val-features fit) but the ladder never ran it — the fairness rule kept it out of the headline and the study closed at 8/8. One clearly-labeled run would price exactly what the "Kaggle-style" peek is worth at 58k rows. Prediction to falsify: +0.001–0.003 over E3's 0.668271, still below E2.
  4. Entity embeddings for region_code. The one higher-cardinality column (22 levels) was one-hot everywhere here. A small nn.Embedding inside the E2 MLP tests the embeddings-beat-encoding claim far below its usual >1000-level rule — cheap, and it upgrades the architecture that actually won.
  5. Productionize the imputer under MAR, not just MCAR. E7's 3.4× win was measured under uniformly-random masking. Real missingness is MAR-at-best (spec fields absent conditional on model/segment). Re-run the E7 harness with MAR injection keyed on model before recommending the DAE-imputer for a production data-quality workflow.
