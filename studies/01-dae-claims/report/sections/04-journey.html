E1 — the gate (0.625462, keep)

Before any DAE work, the split-identity anchor: the campaign's LR+OHE baseline on the fixed split, required to land within ±0.001 of 0.6255 or the whole study stops. It landed 0.000038 off — and exactly matched study 00's own E1 to the sixth decimal, proving both studies share prepare.py and the split byte-for-byte. Every cross-study comparison in this report is legitimate because of this row.

E2 — the "floor" that became the ceiling (0.670616, keep — THE SURPRISE)

E2 was designed as a humble control: a plain supervised MLP (3×256 ReLU) on the DAE's exact 94-dim input encoding, so that E3-vs-E2 would isolate what self-supervision adds with the representation held fixed. The prediction: 0.63–0.65 — above raw LR, comfortably below the GBDT. The observation: 0.670616, +0.045 over the LR floor and half a point of the fourth decimal above the tuned GBDT's 0.6701. The control tied the champion. Twelve seconds of training on a laptop, early-stopped at epoch 15, with the study's best Brier (0.0587) and best decile lift (1.88) for good measure. Two consequences: the pre-registered keep bar for the DAE instantly hardened from "reach 0.6701" to "beat 0.670616" — and a meta-finding was born that outranks the study's official question (Section 5).

The infrastructure dragon — real research has one

E3's first run did not produce a number. It produced SIGSEGV, exit 139, no Python traceback, and an empty log file — the process died below Python at LGBMClassifier.fit, after the torch DAE stage had already succeeded. The armed prediction-collapse guard never fired; nothing Python-level ever ran wrong. Three isolation probes pinned the cause: (A) a torch-free process running LGBM on cached representations — fine; (B) one tiny torch-MPS op, then LGBM, same process — dead; (C) importing lightgbm first — survives toy loads, then dies inside the full-scale torch stage instead. Conclusion: torch and LightGBM each bundle their own OpenMP runtime (libomp) on macOS arm64, and whichever engages OpenMP heavily second segfaults the process; import order only picks the victim. The fix that carried the rest of the study is two-stage process isolation inside one train.py — a torch-only child fits the DAE and dumps caches, the lightgbm-first parent trains the head (Section 6 shows the code). Two ops corollaries came free: set -o pipefail (tee had masked the exit code) and PYTHONUNBUFFERED=1 (block-buffered stdout died with the process, which is why the log was empty).

E3 — the headline verdict (0.668271, discard)

With isolation in place, the RQ1 experiment ran clean: inductive DAE (swap 0.15, fit on 46,873 train-fold rows only), 768-d deep-stack, the campaign's LGBM recipe. Result: 0.668271 — dead-center in the predicted 0.66–0.67 band, 0.0018 below the tuned GBDT, and 0.0023 below E2's plain MLP on the identical encoding. That last comparison is the clean scientific one, and it is the study's climax: with the representation held fixed, self-supervision subtracted value. The unsupervised detour also cost 3× the wall-clock (37s vs 12s). RQ1: NO, as predicted — but against a harder bar than predicted.

E4 — but the DAE did learn something (0.658019, keep)

A plain logistic regression on the frozen 768-d representations scores 0.658019 — +0.0326 over the raw-feature LR floor, closing ~72% of the raw-LR→tuned-GBDT gap with a linear model. RQ3: YES. The representations demonstrably encode nonlinear joint structure a linear model can use. Both things are true at once, and this is the study's most instructive tension: the DAE learned real structure (E4), and that structure is worth nothing on top of plain supervised learning (E3 vs E2).

E5 — the sweep that falsified its own prediction (0.668271, discard)

The one sanctioned sweep: swap rate ∈ {0.10, 0.15, 0.25}, every trial logged to a sidecar, one ledger row. The pre-registered prediction — |Δ| ≤ 0.002 across rates, "swap rate is a minor lever at this scale" — was falsified: 0.10 costs −0.0063 and 0.25 costs −0.0041. Jahrer's 0.15 is a genuine local optimum on data 25× smaller than his — the one piece of the Porto Seguro recipe that replicated cleanly. The mechanism is visible in the DAE's own held-out reconstruction loss (rising 0.0154 → 0.0230 → 0.0358 with rate): at 0.10 the pretext task is too easy and applies too little denoising pressure; at 0.25 the input is over-corrupted. The winner (0.15) reproduced E3 bit-exactly — 0.668271 — which doubles as the determinism proof for the two-stage isolation. No improvement over E3, so by sweep rule 7: discard, honestly logged.

E6, E7, E8 — the second acts (Phase 2)

E6 (0.660653, discard): concatenating the raw 94-dim encoding with the 768-d representations — "give the tree everything" — did not merely fail to clear 0.6701; it landed 0.0076 below representations alone. The aux ledger shows why: LightGBM's best iteration fell from 107 to 55. With column subsampling at 0.7 over 862 largely redundant dimensions, the duplicated information dilutes every split's candidate pool and the model overfits earlier. A sharper null than predicted: trees do not want your re-expression of information they already have.

E7 (keep): the same frozen DAE, re-purposed as a missing-value imputer under injected MCAR missingness at 10% and 30%, against a train-fold median/mode baseline. Downstream AUC through a deterministically-refit E3 head: DAE wins at both rates, but barely (+0.0015 / +0.0013). At the cell level it is not close — numeric RMSE 1.02 vs 3.42 (3.4× better), categorical accuracy 90.1% vs 33.4% — the DAE is exploiting exactly the model-derivative redundancy the data card flagged. The predicted "gain grows with missing rate" clause was falsified (flat), because information loss itself (clean 0.6683 → 0.6322 at 30% MCAR) dwarfs the choice of imputer. Verdict: reach for the DAE-imputer when you need the values; the classifier's rank barely cares. (Figures in Section 6, where the write-back code lives.)

E8 (0.480548, discard): the last prior — reconstruction error as a calibration-free claim ranker, predicted lift@10% > 1.0 — was falsified with a twist: lift@10 = 0.934, and the ranker's AUC of 0.4805 means the signal is mildly inverted. The policies the DAE finds hardest to reconstruct — unusual vehicle-spec combinations — are marginally less claim-prone, because claims on this book ride weak exposure/usage signal (subscription length, age, density), not configuration weirdness. The same DAE that imputes 3.4× better than median (E7) is useless as an unsupervised claim flagger (E8): "unusual" and "risky" are different directions on personal-lines policy data.

Total ledger wall-clock for all eight experiments: about a minute. The phase budgets allowed three hours.
